Real Estate Investors Flocking to Temple University Area

Increasing numbers of out-of-state Temple University students have intrigued investors interested in investment properties. The university has seen out-of-state enrollment spike by 35%, making the area a brilliant choice for buying properties to accommodate new residents.

Studies show that only 15% of the Temple University student body originates from Philadelphia, and new graduates make up a large pool of property buyers choosing Philadelphia as their permanent home. Is the Temple University area a great choice for out-of-state investors, too?

The Influx of Out-of-State Investors

Out-of-state investors are flocking to the Temple University real estate market. They find existing properties appealing to students and incoming residents. Investors could also develop more residential properties for incoming students, including condos and affordable housing options.

Assessing the market in Philadelphia shows the cost of living is lower than in some areas. The city could provide semester-based rentals, long-term rental homes, and transitional properties for graduating students who want to stay. Choosing these investments could prove fruitful since a thriving job market attracts long-term residents, and the population growth rate indicates a high demand for more housing.

What Makes Philadelphia So Appealing?

Investors want to diversify their portfolios with several passive income streams. The city offers vibrant neighborhoods with a diverse population and economy. Acquiring residential properties here could give them a strategic advantage and growing property values. But what properties generate the highest returns?

Short-Term Rental Opportunities

Rental properties are great investments since the Philadelphia renter's market remains stable and thriving. With adequate property management, they'll keep tenants in the property generating monthly income. Out-of-state investors could take advantage of such services, reducing maintenance costs, protecting their assets, and playing inactive roles in rental processes.

Many investors are buying and renovating single-family properties. Tenants are attracted to amenities such as proximity to the campus and popular attractions such as the Philadelphia Museum of Art, the City Food Tours, and Philadelphia Urban Adventures. Other amenities may include patios, balconies, and additional storage space inside the property.

Single-family homes with open floor plans accommodate study groups and gatherings. Investing in these residential properties with such amenities could provide a higher return and keep student tenants in the property longer.

Students often split residential properties with roommates, giving them more affordable housing and on-site amenities. For investors, it maximizes the profitability of the home, achieving maximum capacity and more than one income source.

Condos and apartment complexes are valuable choices when renting to college students and families. These communities provide additional amenities like swimming pools, laundry facilities, and grills. Assessing the on-site amenities determines what attracts tenants to the property and how much rental property owners can charge per tenant.

Long-Term Rental Opportunities

Families moving into Philadelphia look for family-friendly neighborhoods. Investors choosing properties that appeal to these buyers consider neighborhoods such as Bella Vista, Chestnut Hill, Fairmount, and Mount Airy. Single-family homes in great neighborhoods must have invaluable amenities, such as large yards, adequate space for growing families, and proximity to the best schools.

The influx of investors choosing Philadelphia includes single-home purchases for these purposes. Families need housing they can afford that provides the right advantages. Assessing properties for long-term rental opportunities starts with these considerations. Tenants need a great place for their kids, better educational programs, and homes that accommodate them comfortably.

Many investors also consider property renovations for resell or house flipping. Assessing necessary property updates determines how much to invest and what is feasible. Historic homes are often outdated and don't have the same appeal as new constructions. However, buyers with an affinity for history may want certain original home features to preserve the history. Thorough assessments define when to renovate and when to restore.

Vacation Rentals and Weekend Getaway Options

Visitors come to Philadelphia because of its historical significance, making it a great vacation option. Investors could purchase rental properties and temporarily set up Airbnbs for tourists staying in Philadelphia. Out-of-state investors have recognized the appeal of Airbnbs and how these properties save travelers on hotel fees. Testing the waters with temporary setups determines if the property is a viable choice as a vacation rental. What properties are terrific choices for vacation rentals?

Developers construct properties such as tiny homes from various designs, including storage sheds, cargo containers, and anything inhabitable. These more modest properties are ideal for couples who want to stay in Philadelphia for a weekend. Investors can choose from these properties, newly constructed homes, or renovated existing homes.

When approaching a vacation rental or Airbnb opportunity, investors evaluate the market based on the demographics of frequent visitors, the homes they rent for vacations, and the projected return on their investment. These assessments also reveal the projected tenant turnaround times, potential profits per stay, and how often to schedule professional cleaning services.

Location, Location, Location

The greatest advantage for out-of-state investors is the property's location. Philadelphia is an incredible city filled with attractions, job opportunities, and extraordinary neighborhoods. It is highly appealing to buyers and renters. In addition to these amenities, Philadelphia investment properties are also close to metro areas, including Washington, D.C., and New York City.

Travelers can enter the city via airports and transportation infrastructures around it. Rental property tenants can easily access metro areas from this location. The ease of travel around these areas and all they have to offer increases the property's appeal and maximizes the owner's profitability.

Considering these factors, investors have discovered that the area is not just a sound investment but highly profitable. Philadelphia offers prime real estate for all purposes, giving investors more flexibility and versatility. When choosing properties, investors consider location to optimize the potential of the investment and get the most from it.

An influx of investors has found new advantages and wealth opportunities in the area. The market presents new investments in short and long-term rentals, vacation homes, and possible renovate and resell options. What the investors wish to accomplish determines what properties present the greatest return. Many investors have found growth potential and maximized profits in the Temple University area of Philadelphia. Examining all options and correlated factors shows investors when to invest and what properties to purchase.
